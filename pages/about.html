About Me

Hello, and welcome.

My name is Julio Mendez. I was born in Central America in 1982 and moved to Las Vegas, Nevada, in 1984 during a hot summer that I still hear stories about today. Las Vegas has been home for most of my life. I graduated from Vo-Tech High School in Las Vegas in 2000 and have spent much of my adult life pursuing education in health, science, and fitness. Over the years, I have earned various certifications and licenses, including personal training and CNA certifications, while continuing to expand my knowledge of health and human performance. My formal education took me to Southern Utah University, where I earned two bachelor's degrees. The first was in Biology with an emphasis in Physiology, focusing primarily on Comparative Physiology. The second was in Chemistry with a Health Emphasis, which included areas such as Medicinal Chemistry and other health-related sciences. After graduation, I attended graduate school in Iowa. I successfully completed much of the program and reached my second year before life presented challenges that made it difficult to continue. Although I did not complete the degree, the experience taught me valuable lessons about perseverance, adaptation, and personal growth. Ironically, despite my educational background in health and science, my own fitness journey did not truly begin until 2018.

At that time, I had reached approximately 270 pounds. I was uncomfortable with how I looked and felt, and I had become so self-conscious that I avoided having my picture taken for several years. Rather than looking for shortcuts, I started with the basics. My workouts consisted primarily of cardio. During my first year, I gradually worked up to 45 minutes of cardio six days per week while performing only 10 to 20 minutes of weight training four days per week. The results were life-changing. By the end of that first year, I had reduced my weight to approximately 188 pounds. Once I returned to Las Vegas, I decided to pursue a new challenge: bodybuilding. As I began increasing my training intensity and building muscle, my weight climbed back to around 205 pounds. During that time, an experienced bodybuilder noticed my progress and offered to coach me. Under his guidance, I eventually reduced my weight to approximately 174 pounds while reaching nearly 6% body fat. The transformation was incredible. I looked better than I ever had before, but more importantly, I discovered a passion for bodybuilding and strength training that continues today.

Since then, I have continued to learn, grow, and improve. Even in my 40s, I am still building muscle, improving my physique, and working toward new goals, including future bodybuilding competitions. One of the biggest lessons I have learned is that knowledge and experience are not always the same thing. Despite my educational background, there were times when I trained harder than I trained smarter. When progress comes quickly, it can be tempting to ignore recovery, overlook warning signs, and assume more is always better. It isn't. Through both successes and mistakes, I have learned the importance of balancing training, recovery, nutrition, and long-term health. This website is where I share those lessons. My goal is to provide practical information, honest experiences, and useful resources that help others improve their health, fitness, and quality of life regardless of age or starting point. Thank you for visiting, and I hope something here helps you on your own journey.
